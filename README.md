# India Private School Enrolment Analysis — Excel Dashboard

## 📊 Overview
An end-to-end Excel analytics project examining private school enrolment patterns across India using government data, with a focus on gender equity and student retention.

## 📁 Dataset
**Source:** UDISE+ (Unified District Information System for Education) 2021-22, Government of India
**Scope:** 36 states and union territories, covering Pre-Primary through Higher Secondary enrolment by gender

## 🎯 Business Questions Answered
- Which states have the highest private school enrolment?
- Is there a gender gap in enrolment, and how severe is it by state?
- At what stage do students drop out the most?
- Which states need urgent policy intervention for gender parity?

## 🛠️ Tools & Techniques
- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables for data summarisation
- 4 chart types (bar charts, funnel-style visualisation)
- KPI dashboard design
- Gender Parity Index (GPI) calculation: `Girls Enrolled / Boys Enrolled`

## 🔍 Key Findings
1. India has **8.83 Crore** students enrolled in private schools (2021-22)
2. **72.6%** of students drop out before reaching Higher Secondary level
3. Rajasthan has the worst Gender Parity Index at **0.70** — only 70 girls enrolled per 100 boys
4. Meghalaya leads with the best GPI of **1.20** — more girls enrolled than boys
5. Uttar Pradesh dominates national enrolment with **2.11 Crore** students (24% of India total)
6. Madhya Pradesh ranks 3rd nationally with **65.2 Lakh** students

## 💡 Recommendations
- Prioritise girl-child education incentive schemes in Rajasthan and Bihar
- Target dropout prevention programs specifically at the Upper Primary → Secondary transition
- Study Meghalaya and Kerala as models for achieving gender parity in enrolment
